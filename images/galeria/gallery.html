An alchemist

2014

Back in 2014, I wasn’t consciously aware of either Adamus Saint-Germain or the Crimson Circle but now I can clearly feel that this painting was inspired subconsciously by Uncle A.

A graphical studio turned me down, because my artworks were far away from a fantasy style that they required. So I became frustrated and wanted to prove them wrong. The idea was simple; I decided to draw a fantasy-style warrior.

Everything was going smoothly but in the middle of the painting process something unusual happened. Even though my fantasy warrior looked perfectly fine from an artistic point of view, I was feeling a huge amount of sadness and dissatisfaction.

I couldn’t stop thinking why I should have painted in fantasy style and pleased someone when it was totally against my artistic vision. It was crystal clear to me that I wanted to paint an alchemist instead of a fantasy warrior.

So I covered all the background with a thick layer of acrylic gold paint in order to hide all the fantasy weapons I initially painted on the back of my warrior and instead of following any concrete idea, I let myself go with the flow…

And as I let go of the battle in my mind and stopped controlling the outcome of the painting process, the energies started to transform:

"The warrior fought and suffered enough, so he put down all of his weapons and became a true Alchemist."


This Alchemist can help you in:
  • Facing your light and your dark side
  • Letting go of your fights
  • Connecting with your Master-self
  • Understanding the alchemy of the Soul

Why do you have multiple artist names?

In the past I couldn’t make up my mind about the artist names I was going to use, so I had tried all of them out to see which one resonates with me the most. You can find my previous artworks online here and there under the names of Aya Kashi, Extee, Cassandra or Essie/ Eliza Stolmar.

Where do you find inspiration?

That’s a difficult question to answer… while I’m daydreaming a number of ideas, shapes and colors pop into my mind.
